# E-Commerce Customer Behaviour Analysis

## Business Problem
An online retailer had rich customer transaction data but no clear picture of who their most valuable customers were, which categories drove revenue, and how discounting affected spending behaviour. The business needed segmentation and category insights to guide marketing and inventory decisions

## SQL Techniques
•	GROUP BY aggregation	
•	CTEs for customer classification	
•	CASE expressions (age group, loyalty tier)
•	Subquery (above-average spenders)	
•	Season and category analysis	
•	TOP N with ORDER BY

## Key Queries Performed
•	Total revenue by gender
•	Revenue and customer count by category
•	Discount and promo code usage analysis
•	Shipping method preference and revenue
•	Payment method breakdown
•	Above-average spenders who used a discount
•	Top 5 items by review rating
•	New / Recurring / Loyal customer segmentation
•	Season-wise revenue ranking
•	Age group (Young / Adult / Senior) revenue analysis

## Key Insights
•	Clothing was the top revenue category by a significant margin
•	Loyal customers (21+ previous purchases) generated disproportionate revenue
•	Discounts were used by a large share of above-average spenders — discounting may be attracting quality buyers
•	Fall was the highest-revenue season, pointing to holiday season planning opportunity
•	Adult customers (21-50) drove the majority of revenue despite being the largest segment

## Recommendations
•	Prioritise Clothing inventory expansion and promotional spend
•	Build a loyalty programme specifically targeting customers with 15+ previous purchases
•	Increase Fall / Q4 marketing budget given seasonal revenue concentration
•	Test targeted discounts for Senior customers — currently underrepresented in high-revenue tier
